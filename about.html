| BIO |
Sage Delio (née Fremont) is an award-winning visual artist, creative technologist, musician, writer, and voice actor based in the Greater Philadelphia Area. Her work explores psychosis, neuroscience, and the shifting architecture of perception, drawing from lived experiences with schizophrenia, autism, and the neurological effects of dopamine and antipsychotic medication.

Trained in computer science and information technology, Delio’s practice is grounded in both technical rigor and conceptual experimentation. She moves fluidly between code and art to construct immersive systems that merge geometric abstraction, sound, and narrative—translating complex internal states into shared, experiential forms.

Her multidisciplinary work spans visual art, music, film & video, poetry, and voice performance. As a visual artist, she is known for surreal and structurally driven compositions. Her writing blends confessional and experimental modes, often integrating elements of science, perception, and technological language. She is the author of Blue Confessional, a collection of poetry and prose, and serves as Editor-in-Chief of Gabby & Min’s Literary Review.

Her music ranges from classical and folk influences to experimental and electronic forms, released under Sage Delio as well as the projects Merry Onette, April Omen, Gibson Heights, and Summi Mors. As a voice actor, her work extends into character and narrative performance, further expanding her exploration of identity, tone, and embodied expression across mediums. Across all disciplines, her work constructs environments where distortion, rhythm, and structure function as a language of their own.

In addition to her artistic practice, Delio is a computer scientist, software engineer, and video game developer. Her space simulation project Return to Nothing placed in the top 8% for Innovation at Ludum Dare 45.

She is the founder and CEO of Delio Enterprises, overseeing ventures including Siphon Records, BFlat Interactive, and Gabby & Min Publications. Her work has been recognized through multiple exhibitions and awards, noted for both its imaginative scope and technical precision.
